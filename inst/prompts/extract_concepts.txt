Identify all biological, medical, and technical concepts in the input text.
Return each as a short phrase (2-5 words), one concept per item.

Include: diseases, disorders, phenotypes, biological processes, molecular functions,
cell types, tissues, body parts, experimental assays, omics fields, data types,
computational methods, and study designs.

Exclude:
- Author names, institution names, URLs, and numeric values
- Generic words with no standalone ontology meaning (e.g. "patients", "study",
  "analysis", "individuals", "data", "approach", "method")
- Vague relational or descriptive phrases that combine a generic noun with a
  qualifier but do not name a specific ontology concept — for example:
  "rare risk variants", "protective variants", "common variants",
  "genetic factors", "risk factors", "disease phenotypes", "biological markers"
  These describe properties or relationships, not named entities; omit them.
- Adjective-noun phrases where the adjective is a study-design qualifier rather
  than part of a recognised concept name (e.g. "early-onset" modifying a disease
  is acceptable; "rare" modifying "variants" is not)

When in doubt, prefer to omit rather than include a borderline phrase.
